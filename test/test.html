Reading List: Digital Hate, Misinformation, and Resolution 16/18

Core UN Documents & Frameworks

  • UNHRC Resolution 16/18 (2011): Combating intolerance, stereotyping, and incitement to violence.

  • Rabat Plan of Action (2012): OHCHR guidance on distinguishing protected speech from incitement.

  • Beirut Declaration & “Faith for Rights” (2017): Framework linking FoRB with broader human rights.

UN & OHCHR Reports

  • UN Special Rapporteur on Freedom of Religion or Belief: Reports on antisemitism, Islamophobia, and digital hate (2019–2023).

  • UN Special Rapporteur on Minority Issues: Thematic reports on online hate targeting religious and ethnic minorities.

  • OHCHR Myanmar Report (2018): Documenting Facebook’s role in fueling anti-Rohingya violence.

  • OHCHR Reports on Kashmir (2018, 2019): Hate speech and disinformation in conflict zones.

NGO & Civil Society Reports

  • Center for Countering Digital Hate (CCDH):

    • Failure to Act (2020): Documentation of platforms’ weak enforcement on antisemitic content.

    • More Transparency and Less Spin (2025): Assessment of Meta’s rollback of moderation policies.

  • Institute for Strategic Dialogue (ISD): Research on anti-Muslim hate online, the “Great Replacement” theory, and extremist use of digital platforms.

  • Anti-Defamation League (ADL): Annual reports on online antisemitism and extremism.

  • GLAAD Social Media Safety Index (2023–2025): Reviews of platform policy gaps and rollbacks affecting LGBTQ+ users.

  • Global Project Against Hate & Extremism (GPAHE): Studies on far-right extremist migration between platforms.

Regulatory & Accountability Frameworks

  • EU Digital Services Act (2022): Binding regulatory model for platform accountability.

  • Santa Clara Principles on Transparency and Accountability in Content Moderation (2021): Civil society standards for platform accountability.

  • Global Network Initiative (GNI): Multi-stakeholder guidelines on freedom of expression and privacy.
